🩺 Clinical Markers Tips

HbA1c Level

Normal: below 5.7%. Pre-diabetes: 5.7–6.4%. Diabetic: 6.5% and above. Get this from your latest blood report.

Blood Glucose Level

Fasting normal: 70–99 mg/dL. Pre-diabetes: 100–125 mg/dL. Diabetic: 126+ mg/dL. Random glucose above 200 mg/dL also indicates diabetes.

BMI Calculation

BMI = weight (kg) ÷ height (m)². Use the built-in "Calculate BMI" button for easy calculation from height and weight.

Hypertension

Heart Disease

Parameter Information & Guidelines

Gender

Biological sex. Hormonal and physiological differences between males and females affect diabetes risk and insulin sensitivity.

Age

Age is a major risk factor for Type 2 diabetes. Risk significantly increases after age 45, though Type 2 is increasingly seen in younger adults.

Hypertension

High blood pressure often co-occurs with diabetes. Insulin resistance can cause both conditions simultaneously, making it a strong predictor.

Heart Disease

Cardiovascular disease and diabetes share common risk factors. People with heart disease have a higher likelihood of insulin resistance.

Smoking History

Smoking increases insulin resistance and inflammation, raising diabetes risk by up to 40% in current smokers compared to non-smokers.

BMI

Body Mass Index is one of the strongest predictors of Type 2 diabetes. BMI ≥ 25 is overweight; ≥ 30 is obese — both significantly raise risk.

HbA1c Level

Reflects average blood glucose over 2–3 months. The gold standard for diabetes diagnosis. Above 6.5% confirms diabetes; 5.7–6.4% is pre-diabetes.

Blood Glucose Level

Fasting or random blood glucose measurement. High levels indicate the pancreas is not producing enough insulin or the body is not using it effectively.
